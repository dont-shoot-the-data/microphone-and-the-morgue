Loud about hate.
Silent about murder.

Every year toward the end of June, California Attorney General Rob Bonta rolls out his Hate Crime in California report at a press conference of its own. A week later, the Crime in California report lands with no event, which breaks down homicide, rape, robbery, and kidnapping arrests by race. That one you have to find for yourself. Let's see why.
What he puts on stage

Six killings in four years.

Across his whole tenure, every hate crime in the state combined produced six murders and ten rapes. The rest is vandalism, threats, and shoving.

FBI homicide files · California · 1981–2022

Who actually kills whom in California.

The June ceremony implies the danger is white hate. His own state's homicide files say the opposite. Most killing never crosses race at all, 75.8% same-race. When it does cross, it runs the other way: Black-on-White outnumbers White-on-Black 2,852 to 1,603. A table that showed this would undercut the ceremony, so the office that prints arrests by race has never once printed it. Pick any two groups.

The tables he never reads aloud

And here is who gets arrested, crime by crime, in California.

His own department publishes felony arrests by race every year, Hispanic kept separate from White: Crime in California, Table 31. He has never quoted a row of it. The white line on each bar marks that group's share of the population, so any bar that clears it is overrepresentation. Pick a crime.
Five years, one choreography

He raises the alarm on vandalism.
He buries the body count.

He gives the hate report an epidemic and a stage, and lets the room picture a villain. His own homicide tables, the ones he never reads aloud, name a different one. The number that makes minorities the victim gets a microphone. The number that makes them the suspect gets buried. Not the calendar. Editorial policy, and the editor is the California Attorney General.
And it is all on the record, in advance. For four straight years, the same June: a standalone hate report he rolls out himself, then the Crime in California report days later with no event. Every cell below links to its source.

How to predict the future.

The pattern makes the 2025 cycle knowable before any of it drops, and it drops within days. The hate report lands in the last week of June; the crime bundle, homicides included, on or about July 1.
